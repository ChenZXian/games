# Candidate Concepts

Game ID: island_supply_command
Title: Island Supply Command
Direction: strategy

## Candidate List

1. Working Title: Island Supply Command
Pitch: Command cartoon convoy boats across a chain of islands and win by starving enemy ports while keeping your own front line stocked.
Core Loop: Draw sea routes, launch supply convoys, reinforce forts, capture neutral ports, and collapse the enemy flagship harbor.
Controls: Tap one owned port then a linked target port to toggle a route, tap abilities from the bottom bar, tap a port to inspect stock and upgrades.
Distinctive Hook: Logistics wins battles because supplied ports auto-fire and unsupplied ports become easy to seize.
Retention: Stage progression with new island layouts, route puzzles, and mastery ranks for fast low-loss clears.
Duplicate Risk: Low versus `territory_swarm` because the pressure comes from convoy flow and stock balance instead of direct node attrition routing.

2. Working Title: Toy Trench Marshal
Pitch: Push toy platoons between trench sectors and rotate morale buffs to break a miniature warfront.
Core Loop: Spend command cards, reinforce trenches, rotate morale banners, and break the enemy bunker line.
Controls: Tap sector, tap neighbor, then choose squad card or morale banner.
Distinctive Hook: Morale waves change combat strength every few seconds, forcing timing-focused tactical pushes.
Retention: Short campaign map with faction modifiers and score medals.
Duplicate Risk: Medium versus `lane_warfront` because it still reads as forward pressure across connected fronts.

3. Working Title: Balloon Bastion Dispatch
Pitch: Send cargo balloons between sky towers through shifting wind lanes to power defenses before raiders arrive.
Core Loop: Redirect wind tunnels, launch balloons, charge towers, and survive raid cycles.
Controls: Tap tower pairs to assign balloons, swipe wind fans, tap boosts.
Distinctive Hook: Wind direction changes make every delivery network unstable.
Retention: High puzzle replay value through hazard variants and perfect-route medals.
Duplicate Risk: Low because the strategic layer is route timing through dynamic wind fields.

4. Working Title: Harbor Blockade Patrol
Pitch: Set patrol loops around a compact sea map and intercept smugglers before they fund the enemy fleet.
Core Loop: Assign patrol routes, scan incoming signals, intercept cargo boats, and upgrade checkpoints.
Controls: Drag patrol loops between buoys, tap intercept skills, tap ports for upgrades.
Distinctive Hook: Information delay means the player reads radar pings rather than direct enemy positions.
Retention: Endless escalation with rare enemy ship classes and rank targets.
Duplicate Risk: Low because it is interception strategy, not conquest.

5. Working Title: Power Grid Panic
Pitch: Restore a collapsing island power grid by dispatching repair teams and routing energy around storm damage.
Core Loop: Claim substations, route engineers, reroute power, and keep civilian districts online.
Controls: Tap node to assign engineers, tap cables to enable or disable energy flow, tap emergency skills.
Distinctive Hook: Energy overloads can cascade across the map if the network is misbalanced.
Retention: Scenario-based campaign with harder storm patterns and city objectives.
Duplicate Risk: Medium versus `switchyard_courier` because both involve network routing, but this is macro grid balancing instead of action routing.

6. Working Title: Clockwork Citadel
Pitch: Run a compact mechanical fortress by assigning crews to gears, barricades, and turret rings during enemy breaches.
Core Loop: Shift workers, power stations, and turret lanes to keep the citadel alive across timed siege waves.
Controls: Tap module to assign crew, tap reroute buttons, tap emergency overdrive.
Distinctive Hook: One workforce pool powers every defense system, so every decision is an opportunity cost.
Retention: Upgrade tree and siege mutators create strong replay loops.
Duplicate Risk: Medium versus defense titles, but distinct because the strategic focus is worker allocation rather than tower placement.

7. Working Title: Caravan Crown
Pitch: Build desert caravan lines between forts and markets while raiders and sandstorms tear apart weak routes.
Core Loop: Open trade lines, escort caravans, stock frontier forts, and buy royal decrees that reshape the economy.
Controls: Tap settlement pairs to form caravan lines, tap escorts, tap decree cards.
Distinctive Hook: Markets spike and crash, so the best route plan keeps changing.
Retention: Meta unlocks for caravan classes and decree decks.
Duplicate Risk: Low because the loop centers on trade logistics, not territorial combat.

8. Working Title: Glacier Beacon Line
Pitch: Hold a frozen kingdom by linking beacon towers that slow a spreading frost swarm.
Core Loop: Link towers, send fuel sleds, pulse beacon bursts, and prevent key keeps from freezing.
Controls: Tap tower links, tap fuel drops, tap beacon pulse skills.
Distinctive Hook: The enemy is environmental pressure instead of a classic army.
Retention: Puzzle-heavy maps with optional rescue goals.
Duplicate Risk: Low because it is a survival logistics map rather than standard RTS combat.

9. Working Title: River Fort Relay
Pitch: Push armored barges through river locks to feed artillery forts before pirate gunboats cut the chain.
Core Loop: Open locks, send barges, repair forts, and capture choke points along the river.
Controls: Tap lock gates, tap route arrows, tap fort commands.
Distinctive Hook: River current direction changes convoy timing and exposes weak links.
Retention: Campaign stars plus unlockable barge hull types.
Duplicate Risk: Low because the map is directional and choke-point driven.

10. Working Title: Coral Fleet Signals
Pitch: Command a reef navy by chaining signal towers that steer squads through maze-like sea channels.
Core Loop: Activate towers, reroute fleets, seize coral strongholds, and deny deep-water ambush lanes.
Controls: Tap signal tower to rotate beacon direction, tap squads to issue burst orders.
Distinctive Hook: Movement authority comes from line-of-signal rather than direct movement taps.
Retention: High mastery ceiling from signal puzzles and speed-clear targets.
Duplicate Risk: Low because navigation authority is the core mechanic.

## Selection

Selected Concept: Island Supply Command
Selection Rationale: It is the clearest strategy fit, it stays distinct from existing lane-push and territory-conquest projects, it matches the available cartoon-light UI workflow, and it can ship as a compact Java game with strong readability on mobile.
